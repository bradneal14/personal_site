About

         As a Philosophy major, I devoted the better part of my time in college to honing my skills of critical thinking and analysis. Curiosity is in my DNA. Asking questions is my bread and butter. My last semester of school I decided to audit Intro to Python – why not? After graduation, it suddenly occurred to me that it was not, in fact, too late to pursue a career in programming. After making some money as a tutor I applied to the most rigorous programming bootcamp I could find – January found me at App Academy.

         App Academy was an incredible and intense experience, and I am now armed with a powerful quiver of tools. The foundational knowledge I gained allows me to bring ideas to life - and perhaps more importantly - gives me the intellectual confidence to welcome mistakes as I continue to learn through experience and improve as a developer.

         In my next chapter, I hope to find a programming environment that balances autonomy, which allows me to grow, with mentorship, which guides me as I do. Generally, I enjoy cross-pollination between ideas and fields; and my experience as an entrepreneurial consultant allows me to contribute on and off the keyboard.
